Question XH-3.2023: Given a fair six-faced dice where the faces are labelled '1','2','3','4','5', and '6'. what is the probability of getting a '1'on the first roll of the dice and a '4' on the second roll ?  \\ 
\solution  Let,\\
\begin{align}
 Pr(X=k)&= \begin{cases}
    p, & \text{success} \\
    1-p, & \text{failure}
\end{cases}
\end{align}
Now, the binomial PMF calculates the probability of obtaining exactly "k" successes in a fixed number of "n" independent trials, each with a probability of success "p": with parameters given below,
\\
\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{parameters for PMF}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
\hline
parameter & value\\
\hline
$n$ & 2\\
\hline
$p$ & $\frac{1}{6}$\\
\hline
$k$ & 2\\
\hline
$1-p$ & $\frac{5}{6}$ \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}\\
Now,\\
\begin{align}
\pr{X=k} &= \comb{n}{k} \brak{p}^{k}
 \brak{1-p}^{n-k}\\
\pr{X=2} &= \comb{2}{2}\brak{\frac{1}{6}}^{2}\brak{1-\frac{1}{6}}^{2-2} \\
        &=  \comb{2}{2}\brak{\frac{1}{6}}^{2}\brak{\frac{5}{6}}^{0}  \\
        &= \frac{1\times 1^{2}}{6^{2}} \\
        &= \frac{1}{36}\\
        &=0.028
\end{align}\\
Hence, probability of getting a '1'on the first roll of the dice and a '4' on the second roll is 0.028
